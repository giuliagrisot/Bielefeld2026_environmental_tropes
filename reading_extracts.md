# Opening Reading Exercise: Short Extracts

Use these extracts for the opening discussion:

> What makes this passage environmental?

Try not to begin by listing only nouns. Look also for atmosphere, movement,
spatial relations, descriptive qualities, weather, sound, and mood.

## Frances Hodgson Burnett, *The Secret Garden*

> There was a laurel-hedged walk which curved round the secret garden and ended
> at a gate which opened into a wood, in the park.

## Kenneth Grahame, *The Wind in the Willows*

> At last he took refuge in the deep, dark hollow of an old beech tree, which
> offered shelter, concealment--perhaps even safety, but who could tell?

## Rudyard Kipling, *The Jungle Book*

> Father Wolf listened, and below in the valley that ran down to a little river
> he heard the dry, angry, snarly, singsong whine of a tiger who has caught
> nothing and does not care if all the jungle knows it.

## L. Frank Baum, *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz*

> Not a tree nor a house broke the broad sweep of flat country that reached to
> the edge of the sky in all directions. The sun had baked the plowed land into a
> gray mass, with little cracks running through it. Even the grass was not green.
